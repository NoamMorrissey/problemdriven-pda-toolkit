# Usage Data Summary — Small Team Task Management Survey

**Source:** Online survey distributed via indie hacker communities, freelancer forums, and small business groups
**Date:** 2026-01-20 to 2026-02-05
**Respondents:** 127 people who self-identified as working in teams of 2-8 people
**Method:** 12-question online survey (Typeform)
**Margin of error:** ±8.7% at 95% confidence

---

## Demographics

- Team size: 2-3 people (38%), 4-5 (35%), 6-8 (27%)
- Roles: Team lead / manager (42%), Individual contributor (34%), Founder / co-founder (24%)
- Industries: Tech / Software (31%), Creative / Design (22%), Consulting (18%), Marketing (14%), Other (15%)

---

## Key Findings

### Where tasks are currently tracked

| Method | Percentage |
|---|---|
| Chat messages (Slack, Teams, WhatsApp) | 64% |
| Personal notes (paper, phone, Notion) | 52% |
| Spreadsheets (Google Sheets, Excel) | 34% |
| Dedicated tool (Trello, Asana, etc.) | 28% |
| Email | 23% |
| Nothing / memory | 18% |

Note: Respondents could select multiple methods. The average respondent uses 2.3 methods simultaneously.

### How often tasks are lost or forgotten

| Frequency | Percentage |
|---|---|
| Multiple times per week | 22% |
| About once a week | 31% |
| A few times per month | 29% |
| Rarely | 14% |
| Never | 4% |

**53% of respondents lose or forget tasks at least once per week.**

### How often duplicate work happens

| Frequency | Percentage |
|---|---|
| Weekly | 11% |
| A few times per month | 27% |
| About once a month | 24% |
| Rarely | 31% |
| Never | 7% |

**38% experience duplicate work multiple times per month or more.**

### Time spent on task coordination per day

| Time | Percentage |
|---|---|
| Less than 10 minutes | 18% |
| 10-20 minutes | 34% |
| 20-40 minutes | 28% |
| 40-60 minutes | 13% |
| More than 60 minutes | 7% |

**Median time: approximately 20 minutes per day on manual task coordination.**

### Why dedicated tools were abandoned (N=67, those who tried and stopped)

| Reason | Percentage |
|---|---|
| Too complex for our team size | 58% |
| Team didn't adopt it consistently | 49% |
| Free tier too limited | 31% |
| Felt like overhead, not help | 44% |
| Onboarding took too long | 27% |

Note: Multiple selections allowed.

### What would make a task tool useful (open-ended, coded)

| Theme | Mentions |
|---|---|
| Simplicity / minimal features | 71 (56%) |
| Shared visibility (everyone sees everything) | 64 (50%) |
| Fast task creation (under 30 seconds) | 48 (38%) |
| No account/login requirement for team members | 31 (24%) |
| Works on mobile without an app | 28 (22%) |
| Free | 27 (21%) |

---

## Statistical Highlights

- **Correlation:** Teams that use 3+ tracking methods simultaneously report higher frequency of lost tasks (r=0.42, p<0.01).
- **Team size effect:** Teams of 4-5 report the highest rate of forgotten tasks (61% weekly), suggesting this is the size where informal coordination breaks down but formal tools feel too heavy.
- **Tool abandonment:** 67 of 127 respondents (53%) have tried and abandoned a dedicated task management tool.

---

## Methodology Notes

- Self-selected sample: respondents opted in via community posts. Likely skews toward tech-aware small teams.
- Survey did not verify team size claims independently.
- "Lost or forgotten tasks" was self-defined by respondents, not measured objectively.
- Open-ended responses were coded by two researchers with 89% inter-rater agreement.
