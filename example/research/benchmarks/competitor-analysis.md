# Competitor Analysis — Task Management for Small Teams

**Date:** 2026-02-08
**Researcher:** Research team
**Scope:** Tools used by teams of 2-8 people for task tracking

---

## Tools Analyzed

### 1. Trello

- **Pricing:** Free tier (10 boards), $5/user/month for Standard
- **Target audience:** Teams of all sizes
- **Approach:** Kanban boards with cards
- **Strengths:** Visual, flexible, well-known brand
- **Weaknesses for small teams:** Board/card/list metaphor adds overhead for simple task lists. Power-ups needed for basic features (due dates on calendar view). Free tier limits push toward paid conversion. Onboarding assumes familiarity with kanban concepts.
- **User sentiment (from survey):** "I just want a list, not a board." "It's great for project planning but too much for daily task tracking."

### 2. Todoist

- **Pricing:** Free (5 projects), $4/user/month for Pro
- **Target audience:** Individuals and small teams
- **Approach:** Task lists with natural language input
- **Strengths:** Fast task creation, good mobile apps, clean UI
- **Weaknesses for small teams:** Primarily designed for personal use. Team features (assignments, comments) behind paywall. Shared projects feel like an afterthought. No team dashboard showing who is doing what.
- **User sentiment:** "Great for my personal tasks, doesn't work for team visibility."

### 3. Asana

- **Pricing:** Free (15 users), $10.99/user/month for Starter
- **Target audience:** Mid-size teams and enterprises
- **Approach:** Full project management (lists, boards, timelines, portfolios)
- **Strengths:** Comprehensive, integrations, reporting
- **Weaknesses for small teams:** Significant complexity. Setup time for new projects is 15-30 minutes. Learning curve for all features. Free tier is generous but the interface assumes project management maturity. Multiple views (list, board, timeline, calendar) create decision fatigue.
- **User sentiment:** "We spent more time managing Asana than doing actual work." "It's like bringing a firehose to water a plant."

### 4. Linear

- **Pricing:** Free (250 issues), $8/user/month for Standard
- **Target audience:** Software engineering teams
- **Approach:** Issue tracking with cycles (sprints)
- **Strengths:** Beautiful UI, fast, keyboard-first, excellent for dev workflows
- **Weaknesses for small teams:** Engineering-centric vocabulary (issues, cycles, triage). Assumes software development workflow. Non-technical team members find it intimidating. Not designed for general task management (marketing, design, operations).
- **User sentiment:** "Amazing if you're a dev team. Completely wrong for us."

### 5. Google Sheets / Notion (DIY solutions)

- **Pricing:** Free
- **Target audience:** Anyone
- **Approach:** Build your own tracking system
- **Strengths:** Flexible, free, no new tool to learn
- **Weaknesses for small teams:** Requires manual discipline to maintain. No notifications or reminders. No accountability mechanism. Quickly becomes outdated. Template fatigue: setting up the structure takes effort and the result is fragile.
- **User sentiment:** "It works for a week, then nobody updates it."

---

## Market Gap

| Need | Trello | Todoist | Asana | Linear | Sheets |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Shared task visibility | Partial | Paid | Yes | Yes | Manual |
| Task creation under 30 seconds | No | Yes | No | Yes | No |
| Zero learning curve | No | Partial | No | No | Yes |
| Free for small teams | Limited | Limited | Yes | Limited | Yes |
| Designed for 2-8 person teams | No | No | No | No | N/A |
| No account required for members | No | No | No | No | Yes |
| Works without installation | Yes | No (app) | Yes | Yes | Yes |

**The gap:** No tool is designed specifically for teams of 2-8 people who need shared task visibility with near-zero setup and learning curve. Existing tools are either too complex (Asana, Trello), too personal (Todoist), too engineering-specific (Linear), or too fragile (Sheets/Notion DIY).

---

## Opportunity

A browser-based shared task list with:
- Task creation in under 10 seconds (name + person + date)
- All tasks visible to all team members by default
- No accounts, logins, or onboarding required for team members
- Zero configuration: works immediately after opening
- Designed for the 2-8 person team that's "too small for project management" but too big for informal coordination

This sits in the space between "a chat message" and "a project management tool" — a space that no current product explicitly owns.
