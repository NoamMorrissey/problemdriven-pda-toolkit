# User Interview 02 — James, Freelance Developer

**Date:** 2026-02-12
**Interviewer:** Research team
**Format:** Video call, 40 minutes
**Participant:** James, 29, freelance full-stack developer in London, manages 2-3 subcontractors per project
**Consent:** Recorded with permission

---

## Context

James runs client web development projects and subcontracts pieces (design, copywriting, QA) to freelancers he works with regularly. He uses email for client communication, WhatsApp groups for subcontractor coordination, and a personal Notion page for his own tracking.

---

## Key Quotes

**On how he tracks tasks:**
"I have a Notion table where I list everything that needs to happen for each project. But it's my table. My subcontractors don't have access to it, and honestly, I don't want to give them access to my whole Notion workspace just so they can see their tasks."

**On communication overhead:**
"Every morning I spend about 20 minutes sending WhatsApp messages to each person: 'Hey, where are you with the header design?' 'Did you finish the copy for the about page?' It's like being a human task tracker. I'm manually polling everyone for status updates."

**On missed deadlines:**
"About once a month something slips through. Not because anyone is lazy — because the task wasn't visible to the right person. Last week my copywriter didn't know she was supposed to deliver the FAQ copy before the designer started the FAQ page layout. I knew the dependency but I never communicated it clearly."

**On his Notion system:**
"My Notion works great for me but it's not collaborative. I've thought about sharing it but then I'd need to set up permissions, explain the structure, hope they actually check it. It's easier to just message people. Which brings us back to the problem."

**On what simple means:**
"I need something where I can create a task, pick a person, set a date, and they get notified. That's it. If it takes more than 3 clicks to add a task, I won't use it. I've tested Trello, Monday, ClickUp — they all assume you're a 50-person company. I'm 4 people."

**On consequences:**
"The real cost isn't the missed deadline. It's the awkward conversation with the client when I have to explain why the deliverable is late because of an internal coordination failure that shouldn't have happened."

---

## Behavioral Observations

- James showed his Notion table during the call. It had 47 tasks across 3 projects. 12 were marked "waiting" with no indication of who was waiting or for what.
- He scrolled through 3 WhatsApp groups during the interview to find a specific task update. It took him over 2 minutes.
- His workflow is: think of task → write in Notion → message the person on WhatsApp → hope they respond → manually update Notion when they do.

---

## Summary

James has a personal tracking system (Notion) that works for him but creates a gap: the people doing the work don't see the tasks. He bridges this gap manually through WhatsApp messages, which creates daily overhead (~20 min/day), missed dependencies, and occasional client-facing failures. He has tried collaborative tools and found them overbuilt for teams of 3-4 people.
