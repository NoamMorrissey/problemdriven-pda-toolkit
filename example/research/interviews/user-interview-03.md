# User Interview 03 — Priya, Co-founder of a 4-Person Startup

**Date:** 2026-02-14
**Interviewer:** Research team
**Format:** In-person, 45 minutes
**Participant:** Priya, 31, co-founder and operations lead at an early-stage fintech startup (4 people)
**Consent:** Recorded with permission

---

## Context

Priya's startup has 4 people: 2 co-founders, 1 developer, 1 marketing hire. They work from a co-working space 3 days a week and remotely 2 days. They use Slack, Google Docs, and a shared Google Sheet for task tracking.

---

## Key Quotes

**On the Google Sheet:**
"We have a spreadsheet called 'Master Task List'. It has columns for task, owner, status, and due date. In theory it works. In practice, nobody updates it. I update it on Mondays and by Wednesday it's already out of date."

**On why the sheet doesn't work:**
"The problem is that the sheet feels like homework. It's a separate place you have to go to, remember to open, and manually type into. When you're in the middle of a Slack conversation and you say 'I'll handle the API integration', that decision just lives in Slack. Nobody goes to the sheet to log it."

**On the cost:**
"We're 4 people. We should know exactly who's doing what at all times. But I'd say at any given moment, each person only knows about 70% of what everyone else is working on. That 30% gap causes problems at least twice a week. Someone starts something that someone else already started. Or nobody starts something because everyone assumed someone else would."

**On accountability:**
"When everything is in Slack messages and verbal agreements, there's no accountability. You can't point to a task and say 'this was due yesterday' because there's no task — there's a message from Tuesday that said 'let's try to get this done by Friday'. Try. That's not a commitment, that's a hope."

**On what she needs:**
"Something that lives where we already work. I don't want another app to check. I want a shared list that's so simple that adding a task feels like sending a message. Name, person, date. Done. If I could have that as a browser tab that's always open, we'd actually use it."

**On tools tried:**
"Trello was okay but felt like overkill. Cards, boards, labels, power-ups — we used maybe 10% of it. And the free tier kept pushing us to upgrade. Linear is for engineering teams, not for us. We're not writing epics and sprints. We just need to know who's doing what this week."

---

## Behavioral Observations

- Priya opened the "Master Task List" Google Sheet during the interview. It had 23 rows. 8 had "Status" blank. 5 had due dates in the past with status still "In Progress".
- She spent 3 minutes during the call trying to remember if the marketing hire had finished the landing page copy. She checked Slack, found a message saying "almost done" from 4 days ago, and said "I still don't know if it's done."
- Her phone showed 4 different Slack channels for the same team. Tasks were scattered across all of them.

---

## Summary

Priya's 4-person team has a shared tracking system (Google Sheet) that nobody maintains because it requires deliberate effort outside the natural work flow. Task assignments happen in Slack and verbal conversations, creating a persistent 30% visibility gap. This leads to duplicate work and missed commitments at least twice weekly. She has tried structured tools (Trello, Linear) and found them designed for larger or engineering-specific teams.
