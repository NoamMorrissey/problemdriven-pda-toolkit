# User Interview 01 — Maria, Team Lead at a Design Agency

**Date:** 2026-02-10
**Interviewer:** Research team
**Format:** Video call, 35 minutes
**Participant:** Maria, 34, team lead at a 6-person design agency in Barcelona
**Consent:** Recorded with permission

---

## Context

Maria manages a team of 5 designers working on 3-4 client projects simultaneously. The agency uses Slack for communication, Google Drive for files, and no dedicated task management tool.

---

## Key Quotes

**On how tasks get assigned:**
"Most of the time I assign tasks in our Monday standup over Slack. I type something like 'Ana, can you handle the homepage wireframes by Thursday?' and that's it. Sometimes I send it in the group channel, sometimes in a DM. It depends on whether it's urgent."

**On what happens after:**
"Honestly? I forget about it until Thursday. Then I ask Ana if she did it. Sometimes she says 'What wireframes?' because the message got buried under 200 other messages. That's when I realize I should have written it down somewhere."

**On tracking:**
"I've tried keeping a list in Notes on my phone. It lasted about two weeks. The problem is that the list is mine but the work is shared. Nobody else sees my list. So I end up being the bottleneck — everyone asks me what they should be doing instead of looking at a shared list."

**On duplicate work:**
"Last month two designers worked on the same icon set for a client. Neither of them knew the other one was doing it because I assigned it in two separate conversations three days apart. I forgot I had already asked someone. That was 8 hours of wasted work."

**On tools she has tried:**
"We tried Asana once. It was way too complex for us. We spent more time managing the tool than doing actual work. We need something that takes 30 seconds to add a task, not 5 minutes."

**On what she would want:**
"Just a shared list. That's it. Who's doing what, by when. No Gantt charts, no sprints, no integrations. A list that everyone can see and that I can update in 10 seconds."

---

## Behavioral Observations

- During the interview, Maria checked Slack twice to find a task she had assigned last week. Took her 90 seconds to locate it.
- She had a Google Doc titled "Team Tasks" that was last updated 3 weeks ago.
- She mentioned her team is "too small for project management tools" at least twice.

---

## Summary

Maria's core problem is that task assignments happen inside communication channels (Slack DMs, group messages) and are not captured in a shared, persistent location. This creates information asymmetry (only she knows the full picture), duplicated work, and forgotten tasks. She has tried formal tools (Asana) and found them disproportionately complex for a 6-person team.
